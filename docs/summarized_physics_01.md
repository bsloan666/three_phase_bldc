## 📜 Field Notes: Magnetism & Matter## 1. The Speed of Fields

* The Reality: No force or field acts instantaneously across space.
* The Mechanism: Changes in an electric or magnetic field propagate outward as a ripple (an electromagnetic wave) at exactly $c$ (the speed of light).
* Static Fields: A stationary permanent magnet does not radiate energy. Nearby particles sit within an already established, stable field, which creates the illusion of instantaneous force.

## 2. Why Iron is Attracted (Never Repelled)

* Magnetic Domains: Ferromagnetic materials (like iron or steel paperclips) contain microscopic "neighborhoods" of aligned electron spins called domains. Normally, these domains point in random directions, canceling each other out.
* Magnetic Induction: When a permanent magnet approaches, its field forces the paperclip’s domains to shift and flip into alignment.
* Always Attractive: Because the external magnet dictates this realignment, it always forces the paperclip to form an opposite, attracting pole nearest to it.

## 3. Why Most Materials are "Indifferent"

* The Universe Favors Pairs: For most chemical bonds, a stable energy state means electrons pair off in opposite directions, canceling out their individual magnetic fields.
* Diamagnetism (Wood, Salt, Diamonds): Materials with completely filled electron shells. They have zero net magnetic spin and weakly repel magnetic fields.
* Paramagnetism (Aluminum, Rubies): Materials with unpaired electrons (atomic magnets) that lack the structural coordination to form domains. Room-temperature thermal jiggling keeps them randomized, resulting in a microscopic, virtually unnoticeable attraction.

## 4. Alloys vs. Intermetallic Compounds

* Alloys (Mixtures): Materials like brass or 14k gold are "solid solutions." Atoms substitute for one another randomly without a strict recipe or a new chemical structure.
* Intermetallic Compounds (Chemicals): Neodymium magnets ($\text{Nd}_2\text{Fe}_{14}\text{B}$) are true chemical compounds. They require a mathematically precise atomic ratio to force the elements into a brand-new, complex crystalline lattice, which gives the material its immense magnetic strength.
